Age band is display and age-appropriate-experience information only; changing it creates no account, invitation, ownership, role, permission, or automatic adulthood transition. Managed-minor transition remains deferred.

Screens 29–31 must be reviewed together: activation never makes the backup a Circle Head, never satisfies replacement acceptance, and never bypasses the final-Circle-Head block. Screen 30 grants zero authority and is terminal in Milestone One when no Circle Head can approve. Self-activation, automatic succession, incapacity/death determination, and alternate recovery do not exist in Milestone One; the future branch remains deferred.

Screen 27 visible copy: “Kinward does not automatically convert a managed minor profile into an adult account. Minor-to-adult transition rules remain deferred.” Changing or crossing an age band does not automatically create an account, verified identity, invitation, Circle membership, Care Recipient ownership, role, permission, delegation, or adulthood-transition workflow. The frame offers no “Convert to adult account,” “Claim profile,” “Transfer ownership,” “Invite on birthday,” or automatic-account action.

Compound Screens 29–31 state: Harbor has one last active Circle Head attempting to leave or lose the role; no verified replacement has accepted; and no authorized Circle Head is available to approve backup activation. Screen 31 remains blocked, Screen 29 remains unavailable, and Screen 30 calmly states that the action cannot currently be completed. The backup remains dormant or unactivated, receives zero authority, does not become Circle Head, cannot self-activate, and cannot satisfy replacement acceptance. No automatic succession or alternate recovery path is offered.

Any family-visible audit row about Screens 29–31 independently filters event class, Circle, Care Recipient, actor, approver, activation reason, proposed authority, attempted or blocked value, resulting state, and safe display text. Unauthorized details are masked or omitted.
