# Kinward Family Roles

> **Status:** Current core role requirements; D-8 through D-17 closed and verified by targeted systems audit
> **Version:** 0.1
> **Last updated:** 2026-07-16
> **Governing or related decisions:** D-1, D-2, D-4, D-7, D-9 through D-11, D-14, D-16; F-01-R, F-02-R, F-17 through F-19

## Role Model Principles

- Roles describe coordination responsibility, not medical or legal authority.
- Each adult owns their own private personal and well-being information.
- Each Care Recipient owns and controls their medical and care information.
- Circle administration is separate from medical access.
- A spouse, Circle Head, Backup Circle Administrator, or family relationship never grants automatic medical access or delegated authority.
- One adult may hold multiple roles, but permissions remain scoped by Circle, Care Recipient, information type, action, and time.
- Sensitive access must be explicit, reviewable, revocable, and time-limited where appropriate.
- When authority or consent is unclear, disputed, or withdrawn, use the more private option and do not publish.

## Care Recipient

### Purpose

An adult receiving care whose personal, medical, and care information is coordinated in Kinward.

### Rights and Responsibilities

- Own and control their medical and personal information.
- Remain the sole owner of their adult Care Recipient record; joint ownership is excluded from the MVP.
- Allow other-adult access only through explicit role permissions, Shared Management grants, Delegated Management grants, or separately recorded legal authority handled through the approved review process.
- Choose Self-Managed, Shared Management, or Delegated Management.
- Grant, limit, review, and revoke Kinward permissions.
- Approve medical sharing and family-safe updates about themselves.
- Identify a Trusted Decision Contact.
- Retain access and control while able to exercise it.

### Boundaries

- Care Recipient status does not grant access to another Care Recipient's information.
- A Care Recipient does not automatically receive another adult's private caregiver well-being entries.
- Kinward does not determine whether a Care Recipient has legal decision-making ability.

## Circle Head

### Purpose

Manages approved shared Circle administration. A patient and spouse may both serve as Circle Heads, and a Circle may have other approved Circle Heads.

### Responsibilities

- Manage general Circle membership within assigned permissions.
- Manage shared household preferences and non-medical Circle settings.
- Approve or administer general Circle information when authorized.
- Help identify a Backup Circle Administrator.
- See the “No routing lead assigned” state for non-sensitive Shared Household tasks and either assign a Circle-wide Family Coordinator or directly assign the task.

### Boundaries

- Circle Head status does not grant detailed medical access.
- A spouse receives no additional access solely because they are a spouse.
- A Circle Head cannot override a Care Recipient's sharing decision.
- A Circle Head cannot see another adult's private well-being entry without that adult's permission.
- Resolving Shared Household routing does not create Care Recipient management or sensitive-information access.

## Family Coordinator

### Purpose

Keeps the Circle organized and reduces administrative burden.

### Responsibilities

- Propose invitations and role requests for authorized approval.
- Organize general updates, schedules, and task coverage.
- Identify unassigned, declined, or overdue family tasks.
- Receive unassigned, declined, or overdue Shared Household tasks through the Circle-wide Family Coordinator queue.
- Help resolve task routing after a Circle Head assigns the Family Coordinator role.
- Help members understand where they can contribute.

### Boundaries

- Cannot expand their own access or override a Care Recipient's choice.
- Does not receive detailed medical access merely by holding this role.
- Cannot convert family notes into healthcare-team instructions.
- Does not become a Care Recipient manager or gain sensitive access through Shared Household task routing.

## Medical Lead

### Purpose

Coordinates information that came from a Care Recipient's healthcare team.

### Responsibilities

- Record healthcare-team instructions with source and date.
- Maintain approved treatment and appointment details.
- Help organize questions the Care Recipient may choose to ask the healthcare team.
- Share permitted medical summaries with approved people.
- Keep healthcare-team urgent and after-hours contacts current.

### Boundaries

- Access must be granted separately for each Care Recipient.
- Does not diagnose, triage, interpret results, or recommend treatment.
- Does not alter healthcare-team instructions.
- Cannot share detailed medical information outside the approved audience.

## Care Lead

### Purpose

Coordinates practical daily support for Care Recipients and caregivers.

### Responsibilities

- Organize meals, rides, errands, household support, childcare, and check-ins.
- Monitor task coverage and arrange handoffs.
- Coordinate caregiver breaks and practical relief.
- Keep task details limited to what each helper needs.

### Boundaries

- Does not receive detailed medical information by default.
- Cannot infer medical conclusions from support needs.
- Cannot see or disclose a caregiver's private check-in without that caregiver's permission.

## Chemo Care Lead

### Purpose

Coordinates family logistics around chemotherapy or another configured treatment cycle.

### Responsibilities

- Organize pre-treatment, pump-disconnection when applicable, and recovery checklists.
- Assign transportation, meals, household help, and caregiver relief.
- Include healthcare-team instructions only as approved, sourced records.
- Confirm backup coverage for treatment and recovery phases.

### Boundaries

- Access is scoped to one Care Recipient and treatment window.
- Does not create clinical preparation or recovery guidance.
- Cannot change treatment details or healthcare-team instructions.
- Receives only the medical context explicitly granted for coordination.

## Backup Caregiver

### Purpose

Provides temporary approved coverage when a caregiver is unavailable or needs rest.

### Responsibilities

- Accept or decline coverage windows.
- Review only the tasks, contacts, and instructions needed for that window.
- Record completion and handoff notes.
- Use the Care Recipient's approved contact plan when the caregiver chooses to seek help.

### Boundaries

- Access expires with the approved coverage period unless renewed.
- Does not gain permanent access to medical or caregiver records.
- Does not independently interpret symptoms or alter instructions.

## Extended Circle

### Purpose

Allows relatives, friends, neighbors, faith communities, and other supporters to help without entering a Care Recipient's private medical space.

### Responsibilities

- View intentionally shared, non-clinical updates.
- Claim or complete approved practical tasks.
- Offer practical, spiritual, or emotional support according to family preferences.
- Respect privacy and communication boundaries.

### Boundaries

- Cannot access detailed medical information, Patient Check-Ins, treatment notes, test results, private caregiver entries, private documents, prognosis, medications, emergency information, or unshared appointments.
- Sees only the minimum task information needed to help.
- Cannot invite others, change roles, or re-share Kinward content through the product unless a future reviewed capability allows it.

## Managed Minor Profile

### Purpose

Represents a child or teenager in the Extended Circle through an authorized adult without creating an independent account.

### Allowed Participation

- Receive adult-approved, age-appropriate updates through the managing adult.
- Have an authorized adult submit a prayer, message, drawing, voice note, or supervised contribution on the minor's behalf.
- Be included in age-appropriate household coordination without adult caregiving responsibility.

### Boundaries

- Cannot sign in independently.
- Cannot receive medical or emergency alerts.
- Cannot view symptoms, medications, prognosis, emergency information, private documents, or adult caregiver entries.
- Cannot be assigned adult caregiving or medical responsibilities.
- Cannot receive sensitive locked-screen notifications.
- Direct minor accounts are postponed until legal, privacy, consent, and child-safety review supports them.

The authorized adult who manages the profile, required consent, retention, deletion, and transition when the minor becomes an adult remain pre-development decisions.

## Primary Caregiver Designation

The primary caregiver is an adult designation, not an information-ownership role. It may belong to a spouse or another approved adult.

Kinward must:

- Provide the primary caregiver with Caregiver Today.
- Track caregiver capacity, respite, and backup coverage.
- Let the caregiver share a full private entry, a limited status, a request for help, or nothing.
- Let the caregiver request practical support without disclosing the private reason.
- Keep caregiver well-being separate from every Care Recipient's medical information.
- Avoid treating caregiver needs as secondary.

## Care Management Modes

Each adult Care Recipient chooses one mode.

### Self-Managed

The Care Recipient primarily manages their own profile, permissions, information, and updates.

### Shared Management

The Care Recipient grants selected Kinward management permissions to one or more approved adults while continuing to manage the profile.

### Delegated Management

The Care Recipient appoints one or more adult Designated Care Representatives and chooses the permissions each person may exercise.

Available delegation scopes are:

- Manage daily care.
- Complete Patient Check-Ins.
- Access care and medical information.
- Approve Circle updates.
- Manage appointments and treatment schedules.
- Manage Circle membership.
- Manage privacy settings.
- Upload and organize documents.
- Assign caregiving responsibilities.
- Manage notifications.
- Manage emergency contact information.

The Care Recipient may customize scopes or choose “Grant all Kinward management permissions.”

In Self-Managed mode, other adults may retain non-management coordination or care roles such as Family Coordinator, Care Lead, Medical Lead, or Chemo Care Lead, but those roles grant no management access to the Care Recipient's private information. Other-adult access exists only through explicit role permissions, Shared Management grants, Delegated Management grants, or separately recorded legal authority handled through the approved review process. Family relationship alone creates no access.

Every delegation records:

- Who granted it.
- Who received it.
- Permission scope.
- Start date.
- Expiration date when applicable.
- Current status: active, suspended, expired, or revoked.
- Changes and revocation history.

The interface recommends and prefills a 90-day expiration, but the Care Recipient may deliberately choose “Until revoked.” A delegation without an expiration generates a recurring 90-day access-review reminder. The Care Recipient retains access and may change, suspend, restore, or revoke delegation while able to do so. Immediate suspension or revocation removes permissions, preserves audit history, and invalidates active delegated sessions where technically possible. No family relationship, including spouse, creates an exception. Every delegated action records who acted and on whose behalf. Unclear or disputed delegation results in the more private behavior.

## Designated Care Representative

### Purpose

An adult who operates selected Kinward functions on behalf of a Care Recipient under explicit recorded consent.

### Boundaries

- Receives only the granted Kinward scopes.
- Does not gain authority because of marriage, family relationship, Circle Head status, or caregiving work.
- Does not receive legal healthcare decision-making authority through Kinward.
- Cannot exercise expired, revoked, unclear, or disputed delegation.
- Every action must preserve the acting person's identity and the Care Recipient on whose behalf they acted.

## Trusted Decision Contact

A person the Care Recipient wants First & 8th to contact if authority or consent later requires review. This designation:

- Does not transfer Kinward permissions.
- Does not establish incapacity.
- Does not establish legal healthcare authority.
- Does not replace required identity checks or legal documentation.

## Backup Circle Administrator

An adult designated as a dormant contingency role to preserve approved Circle administration if a Circle Head is unavailable. This role is separate from Circle Head and Family Coordinator.

- Receives no usable Backup Circle Administrator permissions while dormant.
- Receives only assigned non-medical Circle administration permissions after approved activation.
- Receives no automatic access to Care Recipient medical information or adult well-being entries.
- Must pass identity and account-ownership verification when designated and strong reauthentication when activated.
- Cannot self-activate solely by claiming that a Circle Head is unavailable.
- Milestone One activation requires an authorized Circle Head's approval, a fresh provider-supported challenge, a recorded reason, and an audit entry. When no Circle Head can approve, activation remains unavailable pending a separately reviewed future recovery process.
- Activation, actions, suspension, and deactivation are audited.
- Cannot be treated as a successor owner without explicit approval and any required legal review.

## Legal Roles and Documents

Legal roles such as Healthcare Agent, Health Care Proxy, Power of Attorney, Guardian, or Personal Representative are stored separately from Kinward app permissions.

Kinward may record that documentation exists and route it for review. Kinward must not:

- Declare a document valid.
- Decide that a person lacks legal capacity.
- Convert a legal label into automatic app authority without the approved verification process.
- Resolve disputed family or legal claims.

Applicable law and verified documentation—not a Kinward role—govern legal authority.

## Role and Delegation Lifecycle

Every role or delegated permission should support:

1. Invitation or nomination.
2. Approval by the person authorized for that scope.
3. Clear explanation before acceptance.
4. Acceptance and identity verification.
5. Recorded consent where delegation is involved.
6. Periodic review for sensitive roles.
7. Expiration where appropriate.
8. Immediate revocation of active access when authorized.
9. Session revocation and privacy-respecting audit history.
10. Preservation of historical actions after future access is removed.

## Relationship Change and Succession Rules

- Separation or another relationship change may remove future access without deleting historical records, audit history, or completed actions.
- A disputed or withdrawn sharing grant defaults to no publication and the more private access state.
- Account succession or access transfer requires explicit approval, identity verification, and any required legal documentation.
- Kinward does not determine legal authority during incapacity or after death.
- The last active Circle Head cannot leave, remove the role, be removed, or be downgraded until another verified adult accepts Circle Head.
- Backup Circle Administrator status never creates automatic succession to Circle Head.
- Milestone One backup activation requires an authorized Circle Head. When none is available, recovery activation is unavailable until a separately reviewed process exists.
- Another adult Care Recipient becomes an active sole owner only after accepting a dedicated verified-email ownership invitation; their pending record contains no private information.

## Remaining Decisions

- Which supporting documents and review process are accepted in each launch region.
- How Kinward handles competing claims of authority or a Care Recipient who cannot revoke delegation.
- How separation, death, guardianship, and succession requests are paused, reviewed, appealed, or denied.
- Who may create, control, archive, or delete a managed minor profile.
- What consent is required for a managed minor's drawings, voice notes, and other contributions.
- Whether legal documents are stored or only their existence and review status are recorded.
- How a Backup Circle Administrator recovers access without gaining unintended permissions.
